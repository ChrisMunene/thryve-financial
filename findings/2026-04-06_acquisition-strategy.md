## Why Mine's Paid Social Results Are Misleading

Mine's paid social creative advertised "$1,000 credit line, no credit check" — a payday loan signal that attracted financially desperate users, not financially curious ones. The 0.24% HQ rate from Facebook reflects **message-audience mismatch**, not a channel that can't reach the right people.

```
Mine's message:                        Better message:
"Get $1,000 instantly,                 "I spent $847 on food last month
 no credit check!"                      and didn't even realize it."
         │                                        │
         ▼                                        ▼
Attracts: financially desperate        Attracts: financially curious
Looking for: free money                Looking for: clarity & control
Intent: get cash now                   Intent: improve spending habits
HQ rate: 0.24%                         HQ rate: testable (target >4%)
```

**The platforms (Facebook, TikTok, Instagram) can reach the right audience.** The creative determines whether they self-select in.

## Acquisition Strategy: Three Phases

### Phase 1: Creator Partnerships (Month 0-6) — 60% of budget

**What:** Partner with 5-8 financial education content creators whose audience matches the target user profile.

**Why it works:** The creator's content is the qualifying filter. Someone who watches a 90-minute Financial Audit or a 15-minute budgeting tutorial has self-selected for financial improvement intent. The product is the natural next step.

**Who to partner with:**

**Tier 1 — Financial education YouTubers (proven channel):**
- Caleb Hammer (1.5M+ subs — already proven at 8.85% HQ rate)
- Graham Stephan (4M+ subs — investing/budgeting)
- Nate O'Brien (1.3M subs — minimalist finance)
- Humphrey Yang (3M+ subs — explainer format)
- Nischa (1M+ subs — young professional finance)
- Mark Tilbury (8M+ subs — money psychology)

**Tier 2 — Finance TikTok/Reels creators:**
- Short-form financial literacy content
- Reaches 22-28 demographic directly
- Lower cost per partnership, higher volume

**Tier 3 — Niche audiences with high overlap:**
- Military finance creators (8% of P+G users bank with USAA/Navy Federal)
- Student finance creators (34% of P+G are active students)
- Online degree communities (SNHU, WGU, ASU Online — top P+G universities)
- Credit building communities

**Partnership model:** Authentic integration, not ad reads. Creator uses the app on camera ("here's my weekly recap"), gets equity or revenue share for aligned incentives. Long-term relationship, not one-off sponsorship.

**Budget:** $25-50K/month
**Expected output:** 250+ HQ users/month
**CAC per HQ user:** $20-40
**Payback period:** 3-5 months

### Phase 2: Referral Engine (Month 3-12) — $0 budget (revenue share)

**What:** Structured referral program triggered after users experience the core value loop (3 weekly recaps).

**Why it works:** Mine data proves it — P+G referrals become P+G at 39% (11x the rate of non-P+G referrals). High-quality users attract high-quality users because they share demographics, interests, and financial goals.

**Program design:**
- **Trigger:** After user completes 3rd weekly recap (experienced the value)
- **Referrer incentive:** 1 month free subscription
- **Referred incentive:** 1 month free trial (extended from standard 7-day)
- **Sharing moment:** The weekly recap is the natural trigger — "I saved $200 this month, here's how"
- **Mechanic:** Deep link from app → friend downloads → auto-connected → both rewarded

**Projected contribution:**
- 5% of active users refer per quarter
- 39% of referrals convert to HQ
- By month 12: referrals contribute 15-20% of new HQ users
- CAC: ~$5 (foregone subscription revenue)

### Phase 3: Paid Social Testing + Organic (Month 3+) — 35% of budget

#### Paid Social with Intent-Based Creative (20% of budget)

**What:** Test paid social with creative that pre-qualifies the audience through the content itself, not through targeting alone.

**Key principle:** The creative must do the same qualifying work that Caleb Hammer's 90-minute video does — compressed into 15-30 seconds. The person who resonates with "I didn't realize I spent $847 on food" is the target user. The person looking for "free $1,000" scrolls past.

**Creative formats (UGC-style, 15-30 second video):**

```
Hook patterns that pre-qualify:

"I tracked my spending for a week and here's what I found"
  → Shows actual weekly recap screen
  → End: "I had no idea I was spending that much on..."

"My budgeting app finally got my categories right"
  → Shows miscategorized then correctly categorized transactions
  → Targets the categorization pain point directly

"Sunday morning money ritual — 5 minutes, that's it"
  → Shows recap flow: review, confirm, done
  → Makes it feel doable, not overwhelming

"I used to be scared to look at my bank account"
  → Non-judgmental design, focuses on feeling
  → Targets pain point: "don't make me feel bad about money"
```

**Creative must NOT:**
- Mention credit lines, loans, or free money
- Promise savings amounts ("save $500/month!")
- Show dashboard screens packed with numbers
- Use financial jargon
- Feel like a bank ad

**Creative MUST:**
- Feel like a real person sharing their experience
- Show the actual product (weekly recap, categories)
- Lead with the feeling, not the feature
- Pre-qualify through content itself

**Test protocol:**
- Run 3-5 creative variants for 4 weeks each
- Measure HQ rate (not CPI or install count)
- Kill threshold: <2% HQ rate → kill that creative
- Scale threshold: >5% HQ rate → increase budget
- A $5 CPI with 0.5% HQ rate = $1,000 per HQ user (terrible)
- A $15 CPI with 8% HQ rate = $187 per HQ user (workable)

**Budget:** $5-10K/month testing, scale only what works
**Target:** >4% HQ rate to justify continued spend

#### TikTok Organic + Spark Ads (10% of budget)

Short-form "here's my spending recap" content is native to TikTok format. Post organic content, boost what performs with Spark Ads. TikTok users in Mine data had 0.55% HQ rate — better than FB/Google, and with better creative targeting this should improve.

#### Organic Compounding (5% of budget + time)

- **ASO:** High ratings from P+G users (4.77/5) drive App Store ranking → more organic installs
- **Content marketing:** TikTok/Reels/YouTube Shorts about personal finance, targeting exact user profile
- **Community:** Discord or in-app community, weekly challenges, peer accountability
- **SEO:** "Best budgeting apps 2026", "Mint alternatives", "YNAB vs [your app]"
- **Reddit:** Genuine engagement in r/personalfinance, r/FinancialPlanning, r/budgetfood

## Key Insight

The acquisition strategy is inseparable from the product strategy. The weekly recap is simultaneously:
1. A **product feature** (helps users manage money)
2. A **labeling pipeline** (feeds the RLHF flywheel)
3. A **retention mechanic** (weekly habit formation)
4. A **sharing trigger** (natural moment to tell friends)
5. A **creative asset** (real recaps make authentic ad content)

The best acquisition channel is a product so good that users do the marketing for you. Creator partnerships ignite the fire. Referrals keep it burning. Paid social is lighter fluid — useful in controlled amounts, dangerous if misapplied.
